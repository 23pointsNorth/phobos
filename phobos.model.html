phobos.model.inertia.calculateMeshInertia(data, mass)¶

Calculates the inertia tensor of arbitrary mesh objects.

Implemented after the general idea of ‘Finding the Inertia Tensor of a 3D Solid Body, Simply and Quickly’ (2004) by Jonathan Blow (1) with formulas for tetrahedron inertia from ‘Explicit Exact Formulas for the 3-D Tetrahedron Inertia Tensor in Terms of its Vertex Coordinates’ (2004) by F. Tonon. (2)

Links: (1) http://number-none.com/blow/inertia/body_i.html
  1. http://docsdrive.com/pdfs/sciencepublications/jmssp/2005/8-11.pdf
Parameters:
  • data (bpy.types.BlendData.) – The mesh object’s data.
  • mass (float.) – The object’s mass.

active and passive interpretation

“passive” – the object stands still but the inertia is expressed with respect to a rotated reference frame “active” – the object moves and therefore its inertia

consistent with 6x6 method :

active – consistent with N’ = (H^T)^{-1} * N * H^{-1} passive – consistent with N’ = (H^T) * N * H

phobos.model.joints.deriveJointType(joint, adjust=False)¶

Derives the type of the joint defined by the armature object ‘joint’ based on the constraints defined in the joint. If the constraints do not match the specified joint type, a warning is logged. By using the adjust parameter it is possible to overwrite the joint type according to the specified joint constraints.

Parameters:
  • joint (bpy_types.Object) – The joint you want to derive its type from.
  • adjust – Decides whether or not the type of the joint is corrected

according to the constraints (overwriting the existing joint type) :type adjust: bool. :return: tuple(2) – jtype, crot
